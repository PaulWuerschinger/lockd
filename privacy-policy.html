LOCKD

Privacy Policy — Last updated: March 13, 2026

Overview

LOCKD is a gym workout tracker designed with privacy at its core. All your data stays on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to external servers.

Data Storage

All workout data, training plans, body measurements, achievements, and preferences are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. No cloud sync, no external databases, no accounts required.

Apple Health Integration

LOCKD can optionally read and write data to Apple Health (HealthKit), including:

  • Body weight measurements
  • Workout sessions (duration, estimated calories)

This integration is entirely opt-in and requires your explicit permission. Health data is exchanged directly with Apple Health on your device and is never sent to any external service.

Screen Time / Focus Lock

LOCKD uses Apple's Screen Time API (FamilyControls, ManagedSettings) to optionally block distracting apps during your workout. This feature:

  • Requires your explicit authorization
  • Only activates when you choose to enable Focus Lock
  • Does not collect, log, or transmit any information about which apps you use or block
  • All app selection tokens remain on-device

Notifications

LOCKD can send local push notifications as workout reminders. These are scheduled entirely on-device. No notification data is sent to any server.

Analytics & Tracking

LOCKD does not use any analytics SDKs, tracking pixels, advertising identifiers, or third-party services. We do not track your behavior in any way.

In-App Purchases

LOCKD may offer optional in-app purchases processed entirely through Apple's App Store. We do not have access to your payment information.

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer any of your data to third parties. Period.

Data Deletion

You can delete all your data at any time through the app's Settings. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data permanently.

Children's Privacy

LOCKD is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

If we update this policy, the changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
